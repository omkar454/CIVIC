# Welcome to CIVIC: The Citizen's User Guide

> [!NOTE]
> This guide is designed to help you, the citizen, navigate the CIVIC platform. Whether you want to report a broken streetlight, check up on neighborhood issues, or use our smart AI chatbot, everything you need to know is right here!

---

## 1. Getting Started & Core Concepts

### How do I log in or create an account?
1. Open the CIVIC platform in your browser.
2. Click on the **Sign Up** or **Login** button on the homepage.
3. Fill in your basic details.
4. Once you log in, your role is set as a **Citizen**, unlocking all reporting features!

### What are the Warning rules? (Account Blocking)
To ensure the platform is used responsibly, we have a strict anti-spam policy:
- If a user submits fake, abusive, or highly irrelevant reports, an admin or officer will issue a **Warning**.
- **IMPORTANT: 3 Warnings = Account Blocked.** If you receive three warnings on your profile, your account will be permanently blocked from submitting any further complaints. Please use the platform responsibly!

## 2. Reporting Issues in Your Area

### How do I submit a new complaint or report an issue?
1. Navigate to the **"Report an Issue"** page from your dashboard.
2. **Add a Title & Description:** Describe the problem clearly. You can also use the **Voice-to-Text** (Microphone button) feature to simply speak your complaint aloud!
3. **Select a Category:** Choose the closest match. Common categories include:
   - **Roads & Infrastructure:** Potholes, broken footpaths, damaged bridges.
   - **Utilities:** Streetlight outages, water supply issues, power cuts.
   - **Sanitation:** Garbage dumps, clogged drains.
4. **Location Dropdown:** Start typing your address on the map search bar, and select the correct area from the auto-completing **Location Dropdown**. This ensures the mapped coordinates are highly accurate and sent to the right department zone.
5. **Upload Photos:** Attach an image of the issue to help officers understand the severity. (See the AI Evidence section below).
6. Click **Submit**. You will receive a unique tracking ID.

### Use of AI in Evidence (Image Verification)
When you upload a photo of the complaint, the system's **AI Vision Engine** automatically scans the image to verify the issue and assess its severity. This makes sure that fake or irrelevant images are filtered out immediately, and real, severe issues get fast-tracked!

### Why does the system say my complaint is a "Duplicate" and what are Votes?
Before you even hit submit, our smart AI checks if someone else has already reported the exact same problem in your area.
- If the system finds a **Duplicate**, it will suggest you **Upvote** the existing issue instead.
- **What are Votes?** Voting is a way to show that a problem affects many people. Instead of 10 people filing 10 separate complaints about the same pothole, 10 people *upvoting* a single pothole complaint pushes it to the top of the officer's to-do list, treating it as a high-impact community emergency!

## 3. Tracking, Statuses & Officer Chat

### What do the different Complaint Statuses mean?
As your complaint moves through the system, you can track it via the live status tags:
- **Open:** Your complaint has been submitted successfully but has not yet been seen by the municipal department.
- **Acknowledged:** An officer has seen the complaint and accepted responsibility for it. They are preparing to take action.
- **In Progress:** Work has actively started on the ground to fix the issue.
- **Resolved:** The issue has been fixed! The officer will usually upload a "Resolution Proof" (e.g., photos of the filled pothole).
- **Rejected:** The complaint was deemed invalid, fake, or unresolvable by the department.

### Can I speak to the Officer in charge?
Yes! Once your complaint is **Acknowledged**, a dedicated **Officer Chat** channel opens up inside the portal. This allows seamless direct messaging between you and the assigned field officer. You can ask for updates, provide extra details, or answer any questions the officer might have about the location.

### Downloading Complaint PDFs
Need a physical copy of your report for a local community meeting, RWA gathering, or your personal records? Go to the complaint details page and click **"Download PDF"**. The system will instantly generate a beautifully formatted official document containing the complaint ID, photos, map location, and status history.

## 5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Who sees my complaints?**
**A:** Your complaints are visible to the public community feed (so others can vote on it) and are securely routed to the exact Municipal Department responsible for fixing it.

**Q: What happens if an issue is ignored?**
**A:** The CIVIC platform uses an **SLA (Service Level Agreement) Engine**. If an officer ignores an issue past its government-assigned deadline, the system automatically flags it and escalates it to higher-level authorities!
